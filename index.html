About the lab: 
Established in May 2021, the Guangzhou Laboratory, with its research and clinical campuses located in Guangzhou International Bio-Island and Datansha Island respectively, is a new type of scientific research institution in the field of respiratory diseases, their prevention and control. As an important part of the national strategic scientific and technological power, the laboratory adheres to the world's scientific and technological frontiers, the main battlefield of the economy, the major needs of the country, and people's lives and health, and focuses on solving the key scientific and technological problems in the field of respiratory diseases, their prevention and control, and forming a comprehensive and innovative research platform and facility clusters combined with the war, to build a key scientific and technological power to deal with respiratory diseases in the field of prevention, early warning and control, and strive to become a global scientific and technological power. The laboratory will strive to become a large-scale comprehensive research base and a source of original innovation with global influence that is breakthrough, leading and platform-oriented.>About the PI:
Dr. Yuan Jiao has published a total of 30 SCI papers with over 2600 citations and H-factor of 20, of which representative works have been published in Cancer Cell, Nature Cancer, Cancer Research, Nucleic Acids Research, Cell Reports, Bioinformatics and other journals. Representative works include analysis and discovery of inter-ethnic heterogeneity characteristics at the pan-tumor scale, discovery of markers of tumor drugs adapted to patients, and screening of novel targets for targeted therapies and immunotherapies. In the past five years, the group has been focusing on bioinformatics and oncology big data analysis as the main research methodology, and is committed to building a bioinformatics analysis framework based on multi-omics data of tumors, so as to provide molecular guidelines for precision medicine and accelerate the translation of research results. Our main research interests include 1) genetic background association of tumor heterogeneity, 2) multi-omics analysis to discover new anti-tumor therapeutic strategies, and 3) single-cell level tumor heterogeneity research.
